Q15. How much does a license cost?

Pricing depends on how the music is used. Most projects fall into one of the following categories:

1. Personal / Creator Use

Starting from $19 per track
For YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, and other organic content without editing or commercial intent.

2. Commercial Use (Brand / Business Content)

$300 – $500 per project
Applies to brand collaborations, sponsored posts, and business-related content.

Includes any partnership-based content, regardless of ad disclosure. If multiple accounts are involved, licensing fees apply per participating account.

Example: If multiple accounts collaborate on a single piece of content, licensing fees apply per account.
• 2 accounts → $500 × 2 = $1000
• 3 accounts → $500 × 3 = $1500

Accounts that directly participate in the creation, editing, or publishing of the content are considered participating accounts. Accounts that only tag, mention, or repost content without direct collaboration are not included.

3. Sync License (Video Embedding & Editing)

Starting from $500 – $1500+
Required when music is edited, embedded, looped, or synchronized with video.

This includes advertising, promotional content, branded videos, and any form of production use.

If your project involves editing, branding, or monetization, it will typically require a Commercial or Sync license.

Final pricing depends on scope, usage, and distribution. Large-scale campaigns and brand partnerships require custom quotes.
