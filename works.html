Credit-Based Usage vs Commercial Licensing

Credit-based or platform-based usage may be suitable for small-scale, non-commercial, or low-risk social content, but it should not be treated as a substitute for a commercial license, sync license, paid advertising license, client-work license, or brand campaign license.

If the music is used by a brand, client, company account, agency, influencer campaign, sponsored post, paid ad, product launch, or business-owned video, choose a license that matches the actual scope before publishing.

How Licensing Works

A clear licensing process helps agencies, brands, producers, and creators move from track discovery to publication without unnecessary confusion.

1. Choose track Start from recommended tracks, the music catalog, or a public reference that matches your visual direction.
2. Define use Clarify whether the project is personal, creator, brand-owned, client work, sponsored, paid ad, or custom use.
3. Select license Choose Personal, Commercial Creator, Sync, or custom licensing depending on the actual project scope.
4. Purchase / ask Buy the standard license online, or contact first when the project has unusual terms or campaign uncertainty.
5. Save proof Keep the receipt, license terms, track title, project name, campaign period, and client or brand context.
6. Publish in scope Publish only within the license scope. New campaigns, reuse, paid ads, or new platforms may need review.
