Avoid Music Licensing Problems Before Publishing

Many creators and businesses use music because it is available online, included in an app, downloaded from a “free” library, or purchased for listening. But that does not always mean the music can be used in monetized videos, paid ads, brand campaigns, AI-generated videos, or client projects.

A music license should match the actual use case: where the content will be published, whether the video is monetized, whether it promotes a business, whether it is used in ads, and whether a client or brand will rely on the final asset.

  • • YouTube videos can be affected by copyright claims and Content ID systems
  • • Social media videos may be muted, restricted, or removed when rights are unclear
  • • Brand campaigns and client work require stronger rights documentation
  • • AI-generated videos still need properly licensed background music
  • • Paid ads and commercial campaigns require clear commercial permission

Official Platform Copyright Policies

Major platforms officially state that purchasing or streaming music does not automatically grant rights for uploading, monetization, commercial use, or social media distribution.

Copyright policies and platform rules may change over time. Always review the latest official guidelines before publishing monetized or commercial content.

FAQ

Can I use Nanashino-chan music in monetized YouTube videos?

Yes, when you choose a license that permits monetized video use. The license should match your platform, content type, and commercial purpose.

Can brands and agencies use this music in ads?

Yes. Sync and custom licensing options are available for brand campaigns, ads, social media campaigns, product videos, client projects, and commercial marketing.

Can I use this music in AI-generated videos?

Yes, if the selected license covers your intended use. This can include AI-generated videos, AI avatar videos, AI marketing content, YouTube publishing, ads, or client-facing commercial projects.

Does buying or streaming music automatically allow commercial use?

No. Purchasing, downloading, or streaming music generally does not automatically grant upload, monetization, advertising, commercial, or social media distribution rights.

Can licensing completely guarantee no copyright claims?

No responsible license should promise absolute platform outcomes. A clear commercial license can reduce uncertainty by documenting permitted use, but platform systems and policies may vary.

Do I need a custom license?

A custom license is recommended for large campaigns, full buyout requests, broadcast use, exclusive usage, agency client packages, or unusual distribution requirements.
