Avoid Music Licensing Problems Before Publishing

Music that is available online, included in a platform library, streamed on music services, or downloaded for listening is not automatically cleared for commercial video use. This is especially important when your content is monetized, published for a business, used by a client, used in paid advertising, or connected to a brand campaign.

A good music license should answer practical questions before publication: where the video will appear, whether the content is monetized, whether it promotes a business, whether it is paid advertising, whether it is client work, and how long the campaign will run.

Why Copyright Permission and Master Rights Permission Matter

When using an existing commercial recording, two different permissions may be involved: permission for the song itself and permission for the released recording. This is one reason why famous songs and existing commercial recordings can be difficult to clear for ads, brand campaigns, client work, and commercial video projects.

FAQ

Can I use Nanashino-chan music in monetized YouTube videos?

Yes, when you choose a license that permits monetized video use. The license should match your platform, content type, and commercial purpose.

Can I use the music in YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Instagram, or LinkedIn videos?

Yes, if your selected license covers the platform and project scope. Organic social media content and paid advertising may require different license types.

Can brands and agencies use this music in ads?

Yes. Sync and custom licensing options are available for brand campaigns, paid ads, sponsored videos, social media campaigns, product videos, client projects, and commercial marketing.

What is the difference between a Commercial Creator License and a Sync License?

The Commercial Creator License is for standard non-ad commercial online content such as monetized YouTube videos, organic social posts, website videos, portfolio videos, and small business videos. A Sync License is for paid ads, brand campaigns, sponsored campaign videos, client advertising, and broader campaign distribution.

Why is Nanashino-chan licensing usually simpler than clearing famous songs?

Nanashino-chan music is original music managed through the Nanashino-chan project. Because permission can be reviewed more directly, the process is usually simpler than clearing famous third-party songs that may involve labels, publishers, PROs, master rights holders, or multiple approvals.

Can I use this music in AI-generated videos?

Yes, if the selected license covers your intended use. This can include AI-generated videos, AI avatar videos, AI marketing content, YouTube publishing, ads, or client-facing commercial projects.

Does buying or streaming music automatically allow commercial use?

No. Purchasing, downloading, or streaming music generally does not automatically grant upload, monetization, advertising, commercial, or social media distribution rights.

Can licensing completely guarantee no copyright claims?

No responsible license should promise absolute platform outcomes. A clear commercial license can reduce uncertainty by documenting permitted use, but platform systems and policies may vary.

What should I do if a Content ID or copyright claim appears?

Contact Nanashino-chan with your Gumroad receipt, track title or track URL, video URL, platform name, and a screenshot of the claim if available. Claim support applies to approved licensed use within scope.

Do I need a custom license?

A custom license is recommended for large campaigns, full buyout requests, exclusive usage, TV broadcast, cinema, games, apps, agency packages, unusual distribution requirements, or long-term commercial use beyond standard license scope.
