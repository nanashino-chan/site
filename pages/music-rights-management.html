What Is Music Rights Management?

Music rights management refers to the process of controlling, protecting, licensing, and tracking how music is used across digital platforms, commercial projects, social media, streaming services, and advertisements.

Proper rights management helps creators, artists, labels, advertisers, and brands avoid copyright disputes, monetization problems, and unauthorized usage.

音楽権利管理とは、音楽の利用許諾・収益管理・著作権保護・利用追跡などを行う仕組みです。

Main Types of Music Rights

Right Type Description
Master Rights Rights to the original sound recording
Publishing Rights Rights related to composition and songwriting
Sync Licensing Permission to use music with video content
Performance Rights Public performance and streaming usage rights

ISRC Codes & Music Identification

Professional music releases often use ISRC (International Standard Recording Code) identifiers.

Each track receives a unique code that helps digital platforms, distributors, streaming services, and copyright systems identify the exact recording being used.

  • ✔ Track-level identification
  • ✔ Used by Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube & distributors
  • ✔ Helps rights management systems track usage
  • ✔ Supports licensing verification
  • ✔ Reduces confusion between recordings

配信楽曲はISRCコードによって個別に管理・識別されます。

Content ID & Automated Copyright Systems

Platforms such as YouTube use automated systems like Content ID to compare uploaded videos against registered audio databases.

If copyrighted audio is detected, creators may receive:

  • ⚠ Copyright claims
  • ⚠ Monetization redirection
  • ⚠ Audio blocking
  • ⚠ Geographic restrictions
  • ⚠ Brand safety concerns

Proper licensing and rights management reduce these risks.

Official Platform Copyright Policies

Major platforms officially state that purchasing or streaming music does not automatically grant upload or commercial usage rights. Commercial licensing may still be required depending on usage.
