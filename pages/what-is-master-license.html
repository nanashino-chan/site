What Is a Master License in Music?

A master license—often called a master use license—is permission to use a specific recorded version of a song. It covers the actual sound recording, not automatically the underlying composition.

Learn who grants the license, who the master licensee is, when video and advertising projects need permission, what an agreement should contain, and why a public-domain song can still have a protected recording.

Direct answer

Master Use License Definition

A master use license is permission from the owner or authorized controller of a particular sound recording to use that recording within an agreed scope. The scope may identify the project, media, platform, territory, duration, campaign, client, editing rights and fee.

The master license answers: “May I use this exact recorded version?” It does not automatically answer: “May I use the underlying song?” or “May I synchronize the music with video?” Those permissions must be confirmed in the agreement or cleared separately.

マスターライセンスとは：特定の録音音源（原盤）を、契約で定めた動画、広告、映画、SNS投稿、ゲームなどに使用するための許諾です。 原盤の許諾だけで、メロディや歌詞など楽曲そのものの著作権、映像と組み合わせるSync利用、すべての商用利用が自動的に許可されるとは限りません。

Master License, Master Use License and Master Recording License

These terms are often used for closely related ideas, but the written agreement—not the label alone—determines the actual permission.

What a Master License Covers—and What It Does Not

A master license can be narrow or broad. Never assume that receiving the audio file means receiving every possible usage right.

It May Cover

  • The named sound recording or version
  • The stated video, film, ad or content project
  • Specified platforms and media
  • A stated territory and license period
  • Approved editing, excerpts or cut-downs
  • The named brand, client or campaign

It Does Not Automatically Cover

  • The underlying composition or lyrics
  • Every future project or client
  • Unlimited paid advertising
  • Broadcast, cinema, games, apps or templates
  • Resale or redistribution of the audio file
  • Exclusive ownership of the music

Master Rights vs Composition Rights

The most important distinction is between the recorded sound and the song embodied in that recording.

Master Recording Rights

The specific studio recording, released audio file, live recording or other fixed performance that the listener hears.

+
Composition Rights

The underlying melody, lyrics, harmony and musical work, often controlled by composers, songwriters or publishers.

Public-domain warning

A Public-Domain Song Can Still Have a Protected Recording

An old composition may be in the public domain because its copyright term has expired. That does not automatically make every performance or recording of the composition free to copy or use.

A recently recorded orchestral performance, piano performance, remaster or other released sound recording may still involve master-recording, performer or related rights. Public-domain status must be checked separately for the composition and the exact recording, and protection rules can differ by country and territory.

  • Public-domain composition does not equal public-domain recording.
  • A new recording can have a different owner and protection period.
  • Copying audio from a CD, streaming service or social post may still require permission.
  • Recording the composition yourself avoids using someone else’s master, but arrangements, adaptations and the intended use still need review.

日本語：古い楽曲の作曲・作詞に関する著作権が切れていても、その楽曲を新しく演奏・録音した音源には、実演家やレコード製作者などの著作隣接権が残っている場合があります。パブリックドメインなのは「楽曲」であり、すべてのCD・配信音源・録音物が自由に使えるという意味ではありません。

Master License vs Sync License

The two permissions work together in many audiovisual projects, but they answer different questions.

Recording side

Master Use License

Question: May the project use this exact recorded version?

Asset: The master recording or audio file.

Typical grantor: Master owner or authorized representative.

Composition side

Synchronization Permission

Question: May the underlying music be synchronized with visual media?

Asset: The musical composition.

Typical grantor: Publisher, composer or authorized representative.

Can one agreement include both?

Yes, when one party controls or is authorized to license both rights layers and the agreement expressly includes them. Independent music can sometimes provide a shorter permission path, but the written terms must still identify the project and granted uses.

When Do You Need a Master Use License?

Start with one practical question: Are you using an existing, identifiable sound recording? If yes, master rights should be checked.

Who Is the Master Licensee?

A music license agreement normally identifies at least two sides: the party granting permission and the party receiving it.

Licensor

The Party Granting Permission

The master owner or a representative authorized to license the sound recording. This may be an artist, label, producer, company or licensing administrator.

Licensee

The Party Receiving Permission

The creator, brand, production company, advertiser, agency, client or other named party allowed to use the recording under the agreement.

What Should a Master License Agreement Include?

A useful agreement identifies the recording and the real project precisely enough that both parties can understand what is allowed.

Identity

Recording and Parties

  • Track title and artist
  • Recording version or ISRC
  • Licensor and authority
  • Licensee and final client

Scope

Project and Distribution

  • Project or campaign
  • Platforms and media
  • Territory and duration
  • Organic, paid or broadcast use

Conditions

Rights and Restrictions

  • Editing and excerpt rights
  • Exclusivity or non-exclusivity
  • Fee, credit and reporting
  • Content ID and claim process

Do not rely on the filename alone

A document titled “Master License,” “Master Sync License” or “Commercial Music License” may grant different rights depending on its wording. Check the definitions, grant of rights, restrictions and project details before publishing.

What Affects Master License Cost and Timing?

There is no universal price. Rights holders commonly evaluate the recording, project and distribution before offering terms.

Recording

Artist profile, track demand, label involvement and whether the requested version is available.

Media

Organic social, paid advertising, online video, broadcast, film, cinema, games, apps or other distribution.

Scope

Territory, term, audience, campaign spend, number of versions, client and exclusivity.

Clearance

Number of rights holders, approval process, sensitive categories and whether both rights layers can be cleared together.

A low-cost download or streaming subscription is not evidence that master use, sync, advertising or client-work rights are included. Ask for written permission that matches the project.

Documentation, ISRC and Platform Detection

A license proves permission within its stated scope. It does not make automated music detection impossible.

Records to Keep

  • License agreement and receipt
  • Track title, artist and recording version
  • ISRC or release information
  • Project, client and campaign name
  • Platforms, territory and term
  • Publication and campaign dates

What an ISRC Does

An ISRC helps identify a particular sound recording. It does not replace a license and does not prove that every use is permitted. The agreement establishes permission; the identifier helps connect that permission to the recording.

Master License FAQ

What is a master license in music?

A master license, often called a master use license, is permission from the owner or authorized controller of a specific sound recording to use that recording within an agreed project and scope. It does not automatically clear the underlying composition or every form of distribution.

What is a master use license?

A master use license permits use of a particular recorded version of a song. For audiovisual projects, it is commonly considered alongside synchronization permission for the underlying composition.

Is a master license the same as a sync license?

No. A master license addresses the specific sound recording. Synchronization permission addresses combining the underlying music with visual media. A video using an existing commercial recording may require both rights layers.

What is a master licensee?

A master licensee is the person or organization receiving permission to use the master recording. The licensor is the party granting permission, usually the master owner or an authorized representative.

Does a master license cover composition rights?

Not automatically. Master rights cover the specific recording, while composition rights cover the underlying melody, lyrics and musical work. The agreement must state clearly if both are included.

Can a public-domain song still have a protected master recording?

Yes. The underlying composition may be in the public domain while a newer performance and sound recording remain protected. Public-domain composition status does not make every recording free to copy or use.

What should a master license agreement include?

It should identify the exact recording, licensor, licensee, project, permitted media, territory, duration, commercial purpose, editing rights, campaign scope, fee, credit, Content ID process and any restrictions.

Do social media videos and ads need a master license?

They may when a specific recording is used. Organic platform use, externally edited videos, business posts, sponsored content, boosted posts and paid ads can have different permission requirements.

特定の録音音源（原盤）を、契約で定めた動画、広告、映画、SNS投稿などに使用するための許諾です。楽曲そのものの著作権やSync利用が自動的に含まれるとは限りません。
