Why Music Copyright Matters

Music copyright affects creators, streamers, brands, advertisers, agencies, and businesses using music online.

Even short videos on YouTube Shorts, TikTok, or Instagram Reels may receive copyright claims if music is used without permission.

SNSやYouTubeでは、 音楽利用に対して自動的に著作権チェックが行われています。

The Two Main Types of Music Rights

🎵 Composition Rights

These rights protect the melody, lyrics, and songwriting itself.

Usually controlled by songwriters, composers, publishers, or copyright organizations.

作詞・作曲の権利

🎧 Master Rights

These rights protect the actual sound recording.

Usually owned by artists, labels, or distributors.

原盤権（録音音源の権利）

Why Music Licensing Becomes Complicated

A single song may involve multiple copyright owners.

  • ⚠ Songwriter
  • ⚠ Publisher
  • ⚠ Record label
  • ⚠ Distributor
  • ⚠ Performance rights organizations

This is why creators often face confusion when using music in videos or advertisements.

How Content ID Detects Music

Platforms like YouTube use automated systems such as Content ID to scan uploaded videos.

  1. 🎵 Rights holders register audio fingerprints
  2. 📺 Creators upload videos
  3. 🔍 Platforms automatically scan the audio
  4. ⚠ Matching content may receive copyright claims
  5. 💰 Monetization rules may be applied automatically

YouTubeではContent IDによって自動照合が行われています。

Common Copyright Misunderstandings

❌ “I bought the song, so I can use it”

Purchasing music does not automatically grant upload, monetization, or commercial rights.

❌ “Giving credit is enough”

Crediting the artist usually does not replace proper licensing agreements.

❌ “Royalty free means no restrictions”

Some royalty-free music may still have platform or commercial limitations.

Official Platform Copyright Policies

Major platforms officially state that purchasing or streaming music does not automatically grant upload, monetization, advertising, or commercial usage rights.

  • YouTube Copyright Guidelines
  • Instagram Copyright Information
  • Facebook Copyright Policy
  • TikTok Copyright Policy

Platform copyright policies may change over time. Always review the latest official guidelines before publishing monetized or branded content.

Commercial Use Requires Proper Licensing

Commercial usage includes:

  • 💰 Monetized YouTube videos
  • 📢 Advertisements
  • 🏢 Brand campaigns
  • 🎬 Sponsored videos
  • 📱 Social media marketing
  • 🎮 Streaming & creator businesses

商用利用では、通常の視聴用途とは異なるライセンスが必要になる場合があります。

ISRC Codes & Music Identification

Distributed music is often assigned ISRC codes (International Standard Recording Codes).

ISRC codes help identify recordings individually across streaming platforms, distributors, and licensing systems.

配信音源はISRCコードによって個別管理されています。

Why Controlled Rights Matter for Creators

Using music with controlled licensing simplifies copyright management for creators and brands.

  • ✔ Original lo-fi music
  • ✔ Commercial licensing available
  • ✔ Controlled master rights
  • ✔ Safer for branded content
  • ✔ Better clarity for creators & agencies

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Spotify music in YouTube videos?

Streaming access does not automatically include upload or commercial rights.

What is a sync license?

A sync license allows music to be synchronized with visual content such as videos, ads, films, or branded content.

Does royalty-free mean copyright-free?

No. Royalty-free music may still have licensing conditions and usage restrictions.
