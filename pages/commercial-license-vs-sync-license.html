Commercial License vs Sync License

A commercial music license and a sync license are not always the same. The right license depends on whether the music is used in organic business content, client work, paid ads, brand campaigns, or synchronized audiovisual media.

Use this guide to understand commercial music licensing, sync rights, synchronization licenses, business video use, paid social ads, client projects, and brand campaign music licensing.

Quick Answer

Choose a commercial license for certain organic business, creator, monetized, or non-ad commercial video uses.

Choose a sync license when music is synchronized with campaign video, paid ads, sponsored content, product launch assets, client campaign deliverables, or broader audiovisual promotion.

  • ✔ Organic business video → Commercial license may fit
  • ✔ Paid social ad → Sync license is usually safer
  • ✔ Brand campaign → Sync or custom license

If your use is a normal organic commercial video, a Commercial Creator License may be the starting point. Why This Difference Matters

Many creators and businesses use the phrase “commercial license” broadly. But music licensing depends on the actual use: where the video appears, whether it is paid media, who the client is, how long the campaign runs, and whether music is synchronized with campaign visuals.

A video used organically on a brand profile is not the same as a paid social ad. A product demo on a website is not the same as a multi-platform campaign. A freelance client edit is not the same as a large brand launch campaign.

What Are Sync Rights?

Sync rights, or synchronization rights, refer to permission to synchronize music with visual media. This is why sync licensing becomes important when music is combined with video, ads, campaign assets, films, product launch videos, apps, games, or other audiovisual works.

Sync rights

Music + visual media

Sync rights matter when a track is placed under moving images, edited to a video, used in a brand film, or combined with campaign visuals.

Master rights

The specific recording

If you use an existing recording, permission may need to cover the sound recording itself, not only the underlying musical composition.

Composition rights

The underlying music

A complete music use analysis may also consider the musical composition, especially when licensing music for commercial audiovisual projects.

Why Both Rights Matter in Music Licensing

When using existing recorded music in a video, brand campaign, client project, or paid ad, the license should clarify the relevant rights for the actual use. Platform availability or casual streaming access should not be treated as full campaign permission.

This is especially important for sync rights because the music becomes part of an audiovisual work.

Platform Audio Is Not a Commercial or Sync License

A track may appear inside Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, or another platform music interface, but that does not automatically mean it is cleared for brand campaigns, paid ads, client work, commercial websites, product launches, or broader audiovisual use.

Platform-library or credit-based usage may be suitable for non-commercial or small-scale projects, but it should not be treated as a substitute for a commercial license, sync license, paid advertising license, client-work license, or brand campaign license.

Commercial License vs Sync License Checklist

Before choosing a license, answer these questions. They help clarify whether your use is commercial, sync, paid media, client work, or custom.

  • Will the music be used with video? If yes, sync rights may matter.
  • Is the video for a business, brand, client, product, or service? If yes, treat it as commercial use.
  • Will the video be posted organically only? A commercial license may be the starting point.
  • Will the video be used as a paid ad or boosted post? Start from a sync license or custom review.
  • Is the video part of a brand campaign or product launch? Sync or custom licensing may be needed.
  • Is an agency or freelancer delivering the video to a client? Confirm the final client usage scope.
  • How long will the campaign run? Campaign duration may affect license choice.
  • Will there be multiple versions or platforms? Multi-platform use may require broader scope.
  • Do you need documentation? Save license terms, receipt, track title, artist name, project name, dates, and usage notes.

Important: A License Does Not Guarantee Platform Behavior

A commercial license or sync license clarifies permission from the rights holder within the stated scope. It does not guarantee how TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, Facebook, LinkedIn, advertising review systems, regional catalogs, account settings, automated audio systems, Content ID systems, or future platform policies will behave.

The safer workflow is to choose the correct license, keep documentation, confirm platform rules, and request custom review when the campaign or distribution is broader than standard online use.

FAQ

What is the difference between a commercial license and a sync license?

A commercial license may cover certain business, monetized, or organic commercial uses. A sync license specifically covers music synchronized with visual media such as videos, ads, brand campaigns, sponsored content, and other audiovisual works. The correct license depends on the final usage scope.

When do I need a sync license?

You may need a sync license when music is combined with video for paid ads, brand campaigns, sponsored videos, product launch videos, client campaign assets, films, apps, games, or broader commercial audiovisual distribution.

What are sync rights?

Sync rights are permissions related to synchronizing music with visual media. They matter when music is used in videos, ads, films, campaigns, online commercials, product videos, apps, games, or other audiovisual content.

Is a commercial license enough for YouTube videos?

A commercial license may be enough for some monetized YouTube or organic online videos if the license scope covers that use. Paid advertising, sponsored campaigns, client campaign assets, or broader distribution may require a sync license or custom permission.

Do paid social ads need a sync license?

Paid social ads often require a sync license or custom permission because the music is synchronized with advertising video and distributed through paid media platforms such as TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, or LinkedIn.

Can platform music replace a commercial or sync license?

Platform-library or credit-based usage should not be treated as a substitute for a commercial license, sync license, paid advertising license, client-work license, or brand campaign license.
