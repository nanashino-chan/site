Why Music Licensing Matters

Many creators assume that purchasing a song or finding “free music” automatically allows commercial use. In reality, platforms such as YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook still enforce copyright rules and platform policies.

What Is Music Licensing?

Music licensing is permission from the copyright holder allowing creators, businesses, or brands to legally use music in videos and media projects.

Depending on the project, licensing may include:

  • ✔ YouTube monetization rights
  • ✔ Instagram & TikTok usage rights
  • ✔ Commercial advertising usage
  • ✔ Brand campaign permissions
  • ✔ Client project usage
  • ✔ Podcast & livestream usage

Common Misunderstandings About Music Usage

  • ❌ “I bought the song, so I can use it in videos.”
  • ❌ “Credit in the description removes copyright risk.”
  • ❌ “Royalty free means no restrictions.”
  • ❌ “Spotify music is safe for YouTube uploads.”
  • ❌ “Short videos do not require licensing.”

Official platform policies and copyright laws may still require proper licensing, especially for monetized content, advertisements, or business-related usage.

Music Rights Can Be More Complex Than Expected

In many commercial music cases, creators may need permission for both:

  • ✔ Sound recording usage (master rights)
  • ✔ Composition rights managed by organizations such as JASRAC

日本では、音源利用許諾だけではなく、 JASRACなどの著作権管理団体への申請が必要になるケースがあります。

Large labels and publishers often separate these rights, which can make licensing more complicated for creators and agencies.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use purchased music in YouTube videos?

Purchasing music does not automatically include upload or commercial usage rights. Licensing permissions may still be required.

What is the difference between royalty free and licensed music?

“Royalty free” does not always mean unrestricted use. Licensing terms still determine how and where music can be used.

Can brands and agencies use this music?

Yes — commercial licensing options are available for branded content, advertising, and agency projects.
