What Is a Commercial Music License?

A commercial music license is permission from the copyright owner allowing music to be used in monetized or business-related content.

This includes YouTube videos, TikTok ads, Instagram Reels, sponsored content, podcasts, livestreams, and client projects.

商用ライセンスとは、 音楽を広告・収益化・企業案件・SNS・動画制作などで 利用するための正式な許可です。

Why Commercial Licensing Matters

Many creators mistakenly assume that purchasing or streaming music automatically grants upload rights. In reality, most copyrighted music still requires permission for commercial or monetized usage.

  • ⚠️ Content ID claims
  • ⚠️ Demonetization
  • ⚠️ Muted or blocked videos
  • ⚠️ Copyright disputes
  • ⚠️ Client project risks

Main Types of Music Licenses

Sync License

Allows music to be synchronized with video content such as YouTube, commercials, films, social media videos, and brand campaigns.

Master Use License

Grants permission to use a specific sound recording owned by the master rights holder.

Performance Rights

Public playback rights managed through organizations such as JASRAC or other performing rights societies.

Platforms Still Enforce Copyright Rules

YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook use automated systems to detect copyrighted music. Even short clips may trigger claims or restrictions.

特に広告・案件動画・収益化動画では、 正式なライセンスの有無が重要になります。

Official Platform Copyright Policies

Major platforms officially state that purchasing or streaming music does not automatically grant upload, monetization, or commercial usage rights.

Why ISRC Codes Matter

Professional music releases often use ISRC codes (International Standard Recording Codes) to identify tracks individually across streaming platforms and rights management systems.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Spotify or Apple Music tracks in videos?

Usually no. Streaming access does not automatically include synchronization or commercial usage rights.

Does royalty-free mean copyright-free?

No. Royalty-free music may still have copyright ownership and usage restrictions.

Do brands and agencies need licenses too?

Yes. Commercial projects typically require proper licensing, especially for advertisements and monetized campaigns.
