Why Music Licensing Matters

Using music in videos without proper permission can lead to copyright claims, demonetization, muted audio, or even video removal.

Music licensing gives creators legal permission to use music in monetized or commercial content.

YouTube、TikTok、Instagram、広告動画などでは、 音楽利用にライセンス契約が必要になるケースがあります。

What Is a Music License?

A music license is an agreement that allows you to legally use music in videos, advertisements, podcasts, livestreams, or commercial projects.

  • ✔ YouTube videos
  • ✔ YouTube Shorts
  • ✔ TikTok & Instagram Reels
  • ✔ Sponsored content
  • ✔ Podcasts & streaming
  • ✔ Commercial advertising

How to License Music for Videos

1. Identify How the Music Will Be Used

Different licenses may apply depending on whether the video is personal, monetized, sponsored, or commercial.

2. Confirm the Rights Holder

Music rights may involve multiple parties, including master recording owners and publishing rights holders.

3. Review License Terms Carefully

Always confirm whether the license covers:

  • ✔ Monetization
  • ✔ Social media usage
  • ✔ Paid advertising
  • ✔ Client work
  • ✔ Global distribution

4. Keep Proof of Licensing

Save receipts, agreements, or licensing emails in case platforms request verification.

Common Licensing Mistakes

  • ⚠ Assuming purchased music includes upload rights
  • ⚠ Using streaming platform songs in videos
  • ⚠ Believing credit removes copyright issues
  • ⚠ Ignoring commercial-use restrictions
  • ⚠ Reusing tracks outside allowed platforms

音楽の購入やサブスク契約だけでは、 動画利用・商用利用が許可されているとは限りません。

Why Licensing Helps Reduce Content ID Problems

Proper licensing helps creators avoid many common copyright issues, especially on YouTube and social media platforms.

  • ✔ Reduced risk of Content ID claims
  • ✔ Safer monetization
  • ✔ Clearer commercial permissions
  • ✔ Easier client approval
  • ✔ Better long-term content protection

Official Platform Copyright Policies

Major platforms officially state that purchasing or streaming music does not automatically grant rights for uploading, monetization, or commercial distribution.

  • YouTube Copyright Guidelines
  • Instagram Copyright Information
  • Facebook Copyright Policy
  • TikTok Copyright Policy

Platform policies may change over time. Always review the latest official guidelines before publishing monetized or commercial content.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a license for YouTube videos?

In many cases, yes — especially for monetized, sponsored, or commercial content.

Does royalty free mean I can use music anywhere?

Not always. License terms may still limit platforms, advertising usage, or client work.

Can brands and agencies license music?

Yes — commercial licensing is commonly used for advertising, campaigns, and branded content.
