What Does Content ID Do?

Content ID is YouTube’s automated copyright detection system. It scans uploaded videos and compares audio against copyrighted music databases registered by rights holders.

If a match is detected, YouTube may automatically apply a copyright claim to the video.

YouTubeでは、アップロードされた動画内の音楽を自動解析し、 登録済み音源データベースと照合しています。

How Content ID Works

  1. 🎵 Music owners register audio fingerprints
  2. 📺 Creators upload videos to YouTube
  3. 🔍 YouTube scans the audio automatically
  4. ⚠ If a match is found, a Content ID claim may appear
  5. 💰 Monetization or visibility rules may be applied

What Happens If You Receive a Claim?

  • ⚠ Revenue may go to the copyright owner
  • ⚠ Ads may appear on your content
  • ⚠ Audio may become muted in some countries
  • ⚠ Videos can face regional restrictions
  • ⚠ Sponsored or client projects may become problematic

特に商用案件・ブランド案件では、 著作権問題がクライアントリスクになるケースがあります。

Common Misunderstandings About Copyright

❌ “I bought the song, so I can use it”

Purchasing music on Spotify, Apple Music, CD, or iTunes does not automatically grant upload or commercial usage rights.

❌ “Giving credit removes copyright issues”

Crediting the artist alone usually does not replace a proper license agreement.

❌ “Royalty free means claim free”

Some royalty-free music may still be registered in automated copyright systems.

Official Platform Copyright Policies

Major platforms officially state that purchasing or streaming music does not automatically grant rights for uploads, monetization, advertisements, or commercial distribution.

ISRC Registered Music

Distributed tracks are individually identified using ISRC codes (International Standard Recording Codes).

This helps platforms and distributors accurately identify recordings and reduces confusion around ownership and licensing management.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Content ID claims be removed?

Proper licensing and ownership verification can help reduce claim-related issues, but platform systems may occasionally require manual review.

Can I use Spotify music in YouTube videos?

Streaming access does not automatically grant upload or commercial usage rights. Separate licensing permissions are often required.

Can brands and agencies use licensed music?

Yes. Commercial licensing options are available for creators, agencies, advertisements, and business projects.
