Important Licensing Note

Nanashino-chan music is not “no-copyright music.” It is music that can be licensed directly from the rights holder for the correct use case.

  • ✔ Creator videos may need different permission from brand campaigns.

Why “No Copyright Music for TikTok” Is a Risky Phrase

Many creators search for “no copyright music for TikTok,” but that phrase can be misleading. Most music still has rights attached to the composition, the master recording, or both. Even when a track is available inside a platform, that does not automatically mean it is cleared for every commercial purpose.

日本語：TikTokで使える音源として表示されていることと、企業案件・広告・クライアントワークで自由に使えることは同じではありません。 商用目的の場合は、動画の用途に合ったライセンス確認が必要です。

When TikTok Music Use Gets Complicated

TikTok music use can be simple for casual posts, but it becomes more complicated when the video has a business purpose, paid distribution, client delivery, brand partnership, or advertising goal.

Creator content

Organic TikTok videos

A creator may post lifestyle videos, aesthetic edits, study clips, or café-style content. If the content is non-commercial or casual, platform rules may be enough, but usage scope should still be checked.

Sponsored content

Brand deals and product posts

If a video promotes a product, brand, shop, app, service, or sponsor, the music use is no longer just a casual creator post. A commercial license or campaign-specific permission may be needed.

Paid distribution

TikTok ads and campaigns

Paid ads, whitelisted posts, boosted videos, campaign creatives, and client deliverables usually require a broader license, such as a sync license or custom permission.

Platform Audio Is Not Always a Commercial License

A common mistake is assuming that music available on a social platform is automatically cleared for all business uses. In practice, the safest approach is to separate platform posting from commercial licensing.

Simple TikTok Use Case Guide

  • Personal aesthetic TikTok: Personal use may be enough only if there is no business purpose, no monetization, no sponsorship, no client work, and no advertising.
  • Creator video with monetization: Consider a Commercial Creator License, especially if the content is part of a creator business.
  • Sponsored post or brand deal: Treat this as commercial use. Check the Commercial Creator License or ask about campaign scope.
  • Product demo or business account post: Commercial licensing is usually the safer starting point.
  • Paid TikTok ad, boosted post, or campaign creative: Start from a Sync License or custom license inquiry.
  • Client work or agency deliverable: Make sure the license scope covers the client, campaign, platform, duration, and media usage.

Why Direct Licensing Helps TikTok Campaigns

Direct licensing is useful when you need clearer documentation, consistent music usage, and a stronger rights basis for business or campaign content.

Documentation

Proof of permission

A license provides a written basis for how the track may be used. This is especially useful for client work, brand approvals, and commercial production workflows.

Consistency

Use the same sound across assets

Brands often need the same music across TikTok, Instagram, YouTube Shorts, websites, product clips, and campaign assets. Direct licensing can make that easier to manage.

Commercial scope

Match the license to the campaign

Campaigns can differ by platform, duration, territory, paid distribution, client involvement, and usage type. The license should match the real campaign.

Important: A License Does Not Control TikTok’s Platform Systems

A music license clarifies permission from the rights holder. It does not guarantee how TikTok, automated audio systems, ad review systems, regional catalogs, account settings, or future platform policy changes will behave.

This is why the safer wording is not “no copyright issues.” The safer wording is: use properly licensed music, keep documentation, and choose the right license before publishing commercial or campaign content.

FAQ

Can I use Nanashino-chan music in TikTok videos?

Yes, if the use fits the license you choose. Personal videos, creator business content, sponsored posts, client work, and paid ads may require different licensing scopes.

Is this no-copyright music for TikTok?

No. Nanashino-chan music is not no-copyright music. It is music that can be licensed directly from the rights holder for specific permitted uses.

Can a license guarantee that TikTok will never mute or restrict my audio?

No. A license clarifies permission from the rights holder, but platform behavior can still depend on TikTok’s systems, regional availability, account settings, advertising review, upload method, and platform policy changes.

Do TikTok brand deals need a commercial music license?

Brand deals, sponsored posts, product promotions, client work, and business account content should be treated as commercial use. Depending on the campaign scope, a Commercial Creator License, Sync License, or custom license may be needed.

Do TikTok ads need a Sync License?

Paid ads, boosted videos, campaign creatives, and wider promotional distribution usually require broader permission than casual posting. A Sync License or custom license is the safer starting point.
