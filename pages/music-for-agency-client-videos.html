Direct Answer: What Music License Do Agency Client Videos Need?

Agency client videos need a license that covers the final client use. The license should not only cover the agency’s editing process. It should reflect where the client will publish the video, whether it will be paid media, how long it will run, and whether it is part of a campaign.

Why Agency Client Videos Need Clear Music Rights

Agency videos often travel beyond the original edit. A client may publish the same asset on Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn, a website, a landing page, a paid ad campaign, an email campaign, or an internal presentation.

If the music license only considers the agency’s internal production use, the final client may later face uncertainty when the video is reused, boosted, archived, republished, localized, adapted, or added to a campaign.

Platform Audio Is Not an Agency Client Work License

A track may be available inside Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, or another platform music interface, but that does not automatically mean it is cleared for agency client videos, paid ads, brand campaigns, client deliverables, website videos, or broader commercial use.

Platform-library or credit-based usage may be suitable for non-commercial or small-scale projects, but it should not be treated as a substitute for a commercial license, sync license, paid advertising license, client-work license, or brand campaign license.

Before boosting, running ads, extending campaigns, or repurposing the asset, confirm whether the existing license still covers the new use.

Agency Documentation Checklist

Keep this information with the project folder before final delivery. This helps agencies, clients, editors, account managers, and media buyers confirm the music usage scope later.

  • Client name: The final brand, company, advertiser, or client using the video.
  • Project name: Campaign name, product name, video title, or internal project code.
  • Track details: Track title, artist name, music URL, version, and selected file.
  • License type: Personal, Commercial Creator, 1-Month Sync, 3-Month Sync, or custom permission.
  • Purchase proof: Receipt, invoice, Gumroad confirmation, transaction record, and purchase date.
  • License terms: The license text, scope, restrictions, permitted use, and usage notes.
  • Platforms: YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn, website, landing page, ads, or internal use.
  • Distribution: Organic, monetized, paid media, boosted, sponsored, UGC ad, campaign, or custom use.
  • Dates: Delivery date, publication date, campaign start date, campaign end date, and renewal notes.
  • Future reuse: Whether the client may reuse, re-edit, localize, boost, archive, or republish the video later.

Important: A Music License Does Not Guarantee Platform Behavior

A commercial license or sync license clarifies permission from the rights holder within the stated scope. It does not guarantee how TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, LinkedIn, advertising review systems, regional catalogs, account settings, automated audio systems, Content ID systems, or future platform policies will behave.

The safer agency workflow is to choose the correct license, keep documentation, tell the client what the license covers, and review the scope again before paid advertising or campaign expansion.

FAQ

Can agencies use lo-fi music for client videos?

Yes, agencies can use lo-fi music for client videos when the music is properly licensed for the final client use. The license should match the client, project, platforms, duration, commercial scope, paid distribution, and campaign use.

What license do agencies need for client videos?

Organic client videos, business videos, website videos, and product demos may start from a Commercial Creator License if the scope covers the final use. Paid ads, boosted posts, sponsored campaigns, and brand campaigns may require a Sync License or custom permission.

Who should buy the music license for an agency client video?

The buyer may be the agency, freelancer, production company, or client depending on the agreement. The important point is that the license covers the final client use, not only the agency's internal production process.

Can agencies use platform music for client videos?

Agencies should not assume that platform music is cleared for client videos, paid ads, brand campaigns, or commercial deliverables. Platform-library or credit-based usage should not be treated as a substitute for a commercial license, sync license, client-work license, or brand campaign license.

What proof should agencies keep for client video music licensing?

Agencies should keep the receipt, license terms, track title, artist name, project name, client name, platforms, campaign dates, usage period, ad versions, and any written permission or correspondence related to the music use.

Does a license guarantee platform approval for client videos?

No. A music license clarifies permission from the rights holder within the stated scope, but it does not guarantee platform behavior, ad approval, monetization approval, reach, Content ID outcomes, regional catalog behavior, account settings, or future platform policy changes.
