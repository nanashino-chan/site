Using Music on YouTube Is Not Always Simple

Many creators assume that buying a song, streaming music, or giving credit automatically allows them to use music in YouTube videos.

However, YouTube copyright rules are stricter than many people realize. Music usage often requires permission or a commercial license from the rights holder.

「購入した曲だから使っても良い」 「クレジットを書けば問題ない」 と思われがちですが、 実際には著作権やライセンス契約が関係します。

What Happens If You Use Copyrighted Music?

  • ⚠ Content ID claims
  • ⚠ Monetization redirected to rights holders
  • ⚠ Ads placed on your videos
  • ⚠ Muted or blocked videos
  • ⚠ Copyright strikes
  • ⚠ Restrictions in certain countries

Even short clips or background music can trigger automated detection systems.

Common Misunderstandings About YouTube Music Usage

  • ❌ “I bought the song on iTunes, so I can upload it”
  • ❌ “Giving credit removes copyright risk”
  • ❌ “Only large creators get copyright claims”
  • ❌ “Short clips are always fair use”
  • ❌ “Royalty free means copyright free”

Official YouTube Copyright Policy

According to YouTube’s official copyright guidelines, purchasing music alone does not automatically grant upload or commercial usage rights. Proper licensing may still be required for monetized or branded content.

▶ Read YouTube Official Copyright Guidelines

Platform policies may change over time. Always review the latest official rules before publishing monetized or commercial content.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Spotify or Apple Music songs in YouTube videos?

Usually no. Streaming subscriptions do not automatically include synchronization or commercial upload rights.

Does giving credit prevent copyright claims?

No. Credit alone does not replace licensing agreements or permissions.

Can I use licensed music in monetized content?

Yes — if the license explicitly allows monetization, commercial usage, or sponsored content usage.
