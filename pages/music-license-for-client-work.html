Why Client Work Needs Clear Music Licensing

Client work is different from personal content. When you create a video for a client, the final asset may represent a business, brand, product, service, sponsor, campaign, agency, or commercial project.

That means the music is not only background sound. It becomes part of the client deliverable. The license should cover the final use, not only the editor’s personal access to the track.

Who Should Hold the Music License?

In client work, one of the biggest questions is who should buy and keep the music license: the freelancer, the agency, the production company, or the client.

Option 1

Freelancer buys the license

This can work when the freelancer is responsible for sourcing the music, but the license must still cover the client’s actual use of the final video.

Option 2

Agency buys the license

This is common when an agency manages production, delivery, documentation, approvals, and music usage records for the client project.

Option 3

Client buys the license

This may be clearer when the client will own, reuse, boost, archive, republish, or distribute the video across multiple brand channels.

The important point is not only who pays. The important point is whether the license covers the final client use: client name, project, platforms, duration, paid distribution, campaign scope, and commercial purpose.

Platform Audio Is Not a Client Work License

A track may be available inside Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, or another social platform, but that does not automatically mean it is cleared for client deliverables, agency projects, paid ads, brand campaigns, business websites, or long-term commercial use.

Platform-library or credit-based usage may be suitable for non-commercial or small-scale projects, but it should not be treated as a substitute for a commercial license, sync license, paid advertising license, client-work license, or brand campaign license.

日本語：SNS上で使える音源として表示されていることと、クライアント案件・広告・ブランド動画で使えることは同じではありません。 納品物として使用する場合は、クライアントの利用範囲に合ったライセンス確認が必要です。

Tell the client what the license covers and what may require a new license, especially if the video is reused, extended, adapted, or advertised later.

Client Work Music License Checklist

Before handing over a client video, confirm the music license scope. This checklist is especially useful for freelancers, agencies, video editors, motion designers, and production teams.

  • Client name: Who is the final client or brand using the video?
  • Project name: What campaign, video, product, or deliverable is the music used in?
  • Platforms: Will the video appear on YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, a website, ads, or internal channels?
  • Purpose: Is it organic content, paid advertising, sponsored content, client promotion, or a campaign asset?
  • Duration: Is the use one-time, ongoing, 1 month, 3 months, or longer?
  • Paid media: Will the client boost the post or run ads with the video?
  • Documentation: Have you saved the receipt, license terms, track title, artist name, and usage notes?
  • Future reuse: Could the client reuse the video later in a new campaign, edit, ad, or platform?

Important: A License Does Not Guarantee Platform Behavior

A music license clarifies permission from the rights holder. It does not guarantee how TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, Facebook, LinkedIn, advertising review systems, regional catalogs, account settings, automated audio systems, Content ID systems, or future platform policies will behave.

For client work, the safer workflow is to use properly licensed music, keep documentation, confirm the usage scope before delivery, and tell the client when a new license may be needed for paid ads, campaign reuse, or broader distribution.

FAQ

Do I need a music license for client work?

Yes, if you are delivering a video, ad, brand asset, product demo, social post, or other commercial content to a client, the music should be licensed for the client project and intended usage scope.

Who should buy the music license for client videos?

The buyer may be the freelancer, agency, production company, or client depending on the project agreement. The important point is that the final client use, platforms, duration, and commercial scope are covered by the license.

Can I use a personal music license for client work?

No. Personal-use licenses are not intended for client work, commercial deliverables, brand videos, paid campaigns, sponsored posts, monetized projects, or business promotion.

What license do I need for client videos?

Organic business videos and non-ad online client videos may start from a Commercial Creator License. Paid ads, brand campaigns, sponsored campaigns, broad distribution, and time-limited campaign use may require a Sync License or custom permission.

Can agencies use Nanashino-chan music for client projects?

Agencies may be able to license Nanashino-chan music for client projects, but the license should match the client, project, platforms, duration, campaign scope, and whether paid advertising is involved.

What proof should I keep for client work music licensing?

Keep the license receipt, license terms, track title, artist name, purchase date, project name, client name, platforms, campaign dates, and any written permission or correspondence related to the music use.

Can I use platform audio in a client project?

Do not assume that platform audio covers client work. Platform audio may be available for certain in-platform uses, but client deliverables, paid ads, brand campaigns, commercial websites, and sponsored content may require separate licensing.
