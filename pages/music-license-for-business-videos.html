Quick Answer: What License Do Business Videos Need?

Most business videos need either a Commercial Creator License, Portfolio Commercial License, Sync License, or a custom license. The correct license depends on how the video is used, where it is published, whether it promotes a business, and whether the content is paid advertising, client work, or campaign-based.

  • ✔ Commercial Creator License — for standard non-ad commercial online videos, monetized creator videos, organic business content, website videos, and small business videos.
  • ✔ Portfolio Commercial License — for approved partner-rate commercial use when Nanashino-chan may publicly reference the project as a portfolio or case study use case.
  • ✔ Sync License — for paid ads, brand campaigns, sponsored videos, client advertising, promotional campaigns, and longer commercial campaign use.
  • ✔ Custom License — for TV, cinema, games, apps, exclusive use, full buyout, unusual distribution, or large-scale media campaigns.

What Counts as a Business Video?

A business video is any video that supports a company, brand, product, service, organization, client, campaign, or professional purpose. It does not have to be a paid advertisement.

A video can still be business-related if it helps a company communicate, sell, educate, recruit, train, explain, build trust, support customers, or promote a product or service.

Business Video Use Cases That Often Need Licensing

Business videos appear in many formats. Some are public marketing videos, while others are internal, educational, sales-related, AI-generated, or client-facing. The license should match the actual purpose and distribution.

Product Demo Videos

Product demo videos usually require commercial or sync licensing because the music supports a product, service, app, SaaS platform, or sales message. Even when the video is educational, it may still be commercial if it helps users understand, trust, or buy a product.

Company Social Media Videos

Business videos posted on Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Facebook, LinkedIn, or X may require commercial licensing if they support a company, brand, product, service, campaign, or professional account. A company social media post can be commercial even if it is not boosted as a paid ad.

Corporate Training and Internal Videos

Internal business videos can still require licensing. Training videos, onboarding content, internal presentations, recruitment videos, and company culture videos support business operations and should not be treated as ordinary personal use.

Paid Ads and Landing Page Videos

Paid ads are among the clearest cases for sync licensing. This includes YouTube Ads, Meta Ads, TikTok Ads, Instagram Ads, Facebook Ads, LinkedIn Ads, landing page videos, product launch videos, and campaign assets.

Client Videos by Agencies, Editors, and Freelancers

Client videos usually require a professional licensing workflow. If an agency, editor, freelancer, studio, or production team delivers a video to a client, the license should match the project, client, platform, and distribution scope.

AI-Generated Business Videos

AI-generated visuals do not remove the need to license the music used in the final video. AI product explainers, AI avatar presentations, AI marketing clips, AI social videos, AI ads, and AI-generated brand videos may require commercial or sync licensing when they support business, promotion, sales, client work, or monetization.

Commercial Creator License vs Sync License for Business Videos

Business videos often involve two licensing questions at the same time: commercial use and sync use. Commercial use focuses on the business purpose. Sync use focuses on music being combined with visual media.

In practice, many business videos need music licensing because the music is used both for a business purpose and synchronized with video. Paid campaigns, sponsored videos, and brand advertising require stronger scope than ordinary non-ad business content.

Why Copyright Permission and Master Rights Permission Matter

When a business uses an existing commercial recording, two permissions may be involved: permission for the song itself and permission for the released recording. This is one reason famous songs and commercial recordings can be difficult to clear for business videos.

Can Businesses Use Music from Social Media Platforms?

Businesses should be careful when relying on music that appears inside social platforms. Platform audio availability and business music licensing are not always the same thing.

A sound may be available to personal users inside an app, but that does not automatically mean it is cleared for business accounts, paid ads, brand campaigns, external editing, client deliverables, or multi-platform commercial use.

Be especially careful with:

  • • Instagram Reels from business accounts
  • • TikTok videos for brands, products, or services
  • • Paid Meta Ads, TikTok Ads, YouTube Ads, or LinkedIn Ads
  • • Videos edited outside the platform and uploaded later
  • • Client videos created by agencies or freelancers
  • • Campaign videos distributed across multiple platforms

If a video supports a business goal, direct commercial or sync licensing is usually a clearer option than relying only on in-app audio availability.

Why Famous Songs Can Be Risky for Business Videos

Famous songs can make business videos feel familiar and high-impact, but they are often difficult to clear for company videos, ads, product demos, brand campaigns, and client projects.

Major-label music may involve multiple rights holders, including record labels, publishers, songwriters, master owners, and collecting societies. A business video may require permission for both the sound recording and the musical composition.

Approval may take time, pricing may vary, and some songs may not be available for advertising or brand use at all. For businesses, agencies, advertisers, and AI video makers, music with clear commercial and sync licensing terms can be more predictable than trying to clear famous songs or using copyrighted music without permission.

FAQ

Do business videos need a music license?

Business videos often need a commercial or sync music license when music is used in company content, product demos, brand videos, ads, client work, corporate videos, recruitment videos, training videos, social media posts, or AI videos that support a business purpose.

What music license do I need for a company video?

The right license depends on the actual use. Internal training, public brand videos, product demos, paid ads, company YouTube videos, social media posts, and client deliverables may require different commercial or sync license scopes.

Is a business video considered commercial use?

In many cases, yes. A business video can be commercial even if it is not a paid advertisement because it may support brand visibility, product awareness, customer education, recruitment, training, company communication, or sales.

Do product demo videos need commercial music licensing?

Yes, product demo videos usually require commercial or sync licensing because the music supports a product, service, app, SaaS platform, brand, or sales-related message.

Do internal company videos need music licensing?

They can. Internal company videos, training materials, onboarding videos, and corporate presentations may still require licensing because they support business operations and are not ordinary personal use.

Can a business use music from Instagram, TikTok, or other platforms?

Businesses should not assume that music available inside a platform is cleared for every business use. Paid ads, brand campaigns, external video editing, company accounts, and client deliverables may require separate licensing.

Do paid ads require a music license?

Yes. Paid ads usually require sync licensing because the music is used with visual media for a business campaign connected to brand messaging, targeting, promotion, and commercial distribution.

Do AI-generated business videos need music licensing?

Yes. AI-generated business videos, AI avatar presentations, AI product explainers, and AI marketing clips may require commercial or sync licensing when music is used with visual media for business, promotion, monetization, or client work.

Can licensed music still trigger Content ID detection?

Yes. Automated systems may still detect licensed music. Clear license records, track information, and project documentation can help reduce uncertainty and support claim review if needed.
