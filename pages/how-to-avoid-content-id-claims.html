What Is a Content ID Claim?

Content ID is YouTube’s automated copyright detection system. It scans uploaded videos and compares audio against registered music databases.

If copyrighted music is detected, the video may receive:

  • ⚠ Monetization claimed by another party
  • ⚠ Ads placed on your content
  • ⚠ Viewing restrictions in some countries
  • ⚠ Reduced monetization opportunities

YouTubeではContent IDによって自動的に音楽照合が行われ、 著作権クレームが付くことがあります。

Common Reasons Creators Get Claims

  • ❌ Using “free music” without commercial permission
  • ❌ Downloading tracks from random websites
  • ❌ Using Spotify or Apple Music songs in videos
  • ❌ Assuming credit alone removes copyright risk
  • ❌ Using licensed music outside allowed platforms

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I avoid Content ID completely?

Using properly licensed music significantly reduces the risk of claims, but platform systems may still occasionally require manual verification.

Does “royalty free” mean claim free?

Not always. Some royalty-free music is still registered in Content ID systems. Always check licensing terms carefully.

Can brands use this music in ads?

Yes — commercial licensing options are available for advertising and business use.
