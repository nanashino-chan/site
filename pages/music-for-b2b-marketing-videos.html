Quick Answer: What Music Can You Use in B2B Marketing Videos?

Use music with clear commercial rights when the video supports a company, product, service, software platform, campaign, sales process, client project, or paid promotion.

B2B marketing videos often move across LinkedIn, YouTube, websites, landing pages, sales decks, email campaigns, webinars, product pages, and paid ads. The music license should match the full business use, not only the first platform where the video is uploaded.

  • • B2B marketing videos are usually commercial or business use.
  • • Product demos and SaaS videos need low-distraction professional music.
  • • LinkedIn videos and company updates should use music with clear rights.
  • • Paid ads, retargeting, and launch campaigns usually need sync or campaign licensing.
  • • Agencies and clients may need proof of music permission.
  • • Keep the receipt, invoice, license certificate, track details, and project records together.

Corporate marketing video is a broad category. It includes brand content, product videos, SaaS demos, app walkthroughs, LinkedIn posts, founder videos, company updates, recruiting content, paid social ads, client campaigns, and sales enablement assets.

Instead of treating each video format separately, B2B teams should start with one parent question: what is the business purpose of the video, and where will the video be used?

For B2B marketing, music licensing should follow the business workflow: strategy, production, distribution, paid promotion, documentation, and future reuse.

What Makes Good Music for B2B Marketing Videos?

B2B marketing music should support trust, clarity, and focus. The viewer may be evaluating a product, company, vendor, founder, service, software platform, or business case. The music should help the message feel polished without becoming the center of attention.

Lo-fi, chillhop, soft electronic, ambient beats, and minimal instrumental music can work well because they create a modern business atmosphere while leaving space for voiceover, captions, UI details, product messaging, and calls to action.

Professional Tone

B2B Marketing Videos Are Usually Commercial Use

A B2B marketing video usually supports a commercial objective. It may explain software, promote a service, support a launch, generate leads, build trust, attract partners, recruit talent, or help a sales team communicate value.

Even if the video is educational, it often still serves a business purpose. That is why music used in B2B marketing should be treated differently from personal background music or casual platform audio.

  • • The video promotes a company, product, service, software, or offer.
  • • The video may appear on a website, product page, or landing page.
  • • The video may be used by sales, marketing, customer success, or recruiting teams.

  • • The video may become a paid campaign, retargeting asset, or launch ad.

Platform Music vs Licensed Music for B2B Marketing

Platform music can be convenient for native social content, but B2B marketing videos usually need more flexibility. A video may start on LinkedIn, then be embedded on a website, uploaded to YouTube, sent in a sales email, included in a pitch deck, or converted into paid ads.

Licensed music is easier to manage because the team can keep documentation that explains the purchase, track, permitted use, and project scope.

Why Both Master Rights and Copyright Permission Matter

A B2B marketing video uses a finished recording as part of a business message. Depending on the music source, commercial use can involve permission connected to the master recording and permission connected to the underlying musical work.

This matters because B2B videos often move across many contexts: LinkedIn, YouTube, websites, sales decks, webinars, email campaigns, product pages, event videos, and paid ads. The music permission should be clear enough for that workflow.

Master Recording

Permission related to the actual recorded sound file used in the B2B marketing video.

Composition Rights

Permission related to the underlying musical work, melody, composition, and copyright.

Usage Scope

Permission should match the actual use: organic business video, website asset, sales content, or paid campaign.

Commercial Creator License vs Sync License for B2B Marketing Videos

The right license depends on how the video will be used. A simple organic LinkedIn post is different from a paid lead generation campaign, retargeting ad, product launch, or agency-delivered client asset.

For organic B2B videos, product demos, LinkedIn posts, website videos, YouTube uploads, sales explainers, and business content, a Commercial Creator License may be appropriate if the use fits the scope. For paid ads, launch campaigns, media buys, and time-limited advertising, a Sync License or custom campaign license is usually safer.

Product or SaaS demo B2B product marketing, feature walkthrough, or software explanation. Commercial Creator License or custom review depending on distribution.

Agency-produced B2B campaign Client deliverable, campaign video package, or multi-platform marketing asset. Commercial or sync license depending on client use and distribution.

What Can Go Wrong With Unclear Music in B2B Marketing Videos?

Unclear music rights can create problems across marketing, sales, paid media, client delivery, and future reuse. The risk often appears after the video has already been approved, embedded, scheduled, or sent to a client.

  • • A website video may not be covered by music taken from a social platform.
  • • A paid ad may require different rights than an organic business post.
  • • A client may ask for proof of music permission after delivery.
  • • An agency may need documentation for campaign files.
  • • A sales team may reuse the video in decks, emails, webinars, or demos.
  • • A product launch campaign may need multiple cutdowns across several platforms.
  • • A team may not know whether the music can be reused in future campaigns.
  • • A video may need emergency audio replacement before launch.

For B2B marketing teams, music licensing is part of campaign infrastructure. It should be clear enough for websites, sales assets, social posts, paid ads, client delivery, and future reuse.

B2B Video Music License Documentation

B2B marketing videos often involve many stakeholders: marketing teams, founders, product marketers, sales teams, designers, agencies, freelancers, legal reviewers, clients, and paid media teams.

Good documentation does not guarantee platform behavior, but it creates a clearer record of what music was used, where it was used, and what permission was obtained.

  • • License file or license terms
  • • Receipt or invoice
  • • License certificate PDF if available
  • • Track title and artist name
  • • Company, client, product, or campaign name
  • • Video title or project URL
  • • Platform list where the video will appear
  • • Website, sales deck, webinar, or email campaign notes
  • • Campaign period or posting dates
  • • Notes about organic, boosted, paid, or reused content

Frequently Asked Questions

Do B2B marketing videos need licensed music?

B2B marketing videos usually need music with clear commercial rights because they promote a company, product, service, software platform, campaign, or business offer. Music used in product demos, LinkedIn videos, websites, sales assets, and paid campaigns should match the actual business use.

What kind of music works best for B2B marketing videos?

B2B marketing videos usually work best with professional, low-distraction instrumental music. Lo-fi, chillhop, soft electronic, ambient, and minimal beats can support product clarity, brand trust, sales messaging, and viewer focus without overpowering the content.

Can businesses use platform music in B2B marketing videos?

Businesses should not assume that music available inside Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, or another platform is automatically cleared for B2B marketing videos. Platform music may be limited to certain in-app uses and may not cover websites, LinkedIn videos, sales assets, paid ads, or client work.

What music license is best for B2B marketing videos?

For organic B2B videos, product demos, LinkedIn posts, website videos, and business content, a Commercial Creator License may be appropriate if the use fits the license scope. For paid ads, launch campaigns, media buys, or time-limited advertising, a Sync License or custom campaign license is usually safer.

Can one music license cover LinkedIn, YouTube, websites, and sales assets?

Only if the license scope includes those uses. A B2B marketing video may appear on LinkedIn, YouTube, a website, landing page, sales deck, email campaign, webinar, and paid ad, so the license should match the full distribution plan.

Can agencies use licensed music in client B2B marketing videos?

Yes, if the license scope allows agency and client work. Agencies should keep documentation showing the license, receipt or invoice, track information, project name, client use, platform list, and campaign scope.

Can B2B video music be reused in paid ads?

Only if the license scope allows paid advertising use. Organic business video use and paid ad distribution can be different licensing situations, so a Sync License or campaign-specific license may be needed for ads.

What proof should businesses keep for B2B video music?

Businesses should keep the license file, receipt or invoice, license certificate if available, track title, artist name, project name, campaign name, platform list, project URL, usage dates, and notes about permitted or excluded use.
