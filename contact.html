Important: Platform Audio and Commercial Licensing Are Not the Same

A track being available inside Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, or another platform interface does not automatically mean it is cleared for paid ads, client work, brand campaigns, business social media, website videos, or broader commercial use.

Platform-library or credit-based usage may be suitable for non-commercial or small-scale projects, but it should not be treated as a substitute for a commercial license, sync license, paid advertising license, client-work license, or brand campaign license.

FAQ

Can I request a license for YouTube, TikTok, or Instagram?

Yes. You can request a license for YouTube videos, YouTube Shorts, TikTok videos, Instagram Reels, Facebook videos, LinkedIn videos, X posts, and other online content. The correct license depends on whether the use is personal, monetized, business-owned, paid, sponsored, or client-based.

Can brands or agencies request a sync license?

Yes. Brands, agencies, marketers, editors, and production teams can request sync licenses for ads, brand campaigns, product videos, paid social campaigns, sponsored content, and commercial online video projects.

Does a license guarantee that no Content ID claim will ever appear?

No. A license clarifies permission from the rights holder within the stated scope, but it does not guarantee platform behavior, Content ID outcomes, ad approval, monetization approval, regional catalog behavior, account settings, or future platform policy changes.

Can I use Nanashino-chan music in paid ads?

Paid advertising usually requires a Sync License or custom permission. Please include the campaign platform, usage period, brand or client name, territory, and whether the ad will run on YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn, X, or another channel.

Can I ask which license fits before buying?

Yes. If you are unsure whether your project fits the Commercial Creator License, Sync License, or Custom License, send an email with your project details before purchase.
